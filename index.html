Biography

Forest genetic improvement is a time-consuming and labor-intensive work, and at present, the improvement level of the main timber species in China lags behind that of developed countries. The use of state-of-the-art technology can speed up the improvement of economic traits and, combined with reasonable cultivation and management programs, can satisfy people’s needs for forest products to the maximum. The core purpose of forest genetic improvement is to maximize genetic gain. The approach is to shorten the breeding cycle, improve the selection intensity and improve the accuracy of genetic information estimates. My PhD research work focused on the establishment of pedigree reconstruction, spatial and competition analysis based genetic improvement system. Mixed linear model is a common tool in data processing. I am interested in the development and application of the model. In addition, genomic selection is currently a hot area in animal and plant genetic improvement and has many advantages over conventional selection strategies. How to introduce this method and optimize the domestic forest genetic improvement project in China is also of interest to me.
